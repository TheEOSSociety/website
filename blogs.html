Blogs of the events

The Relevance of United Nations in the world

The debate revolved around the United Nations' continued relevance in the modern era, with some arguing that its singular achievement of preventing a third world war remains a testament to its enduring importance. Notably, organizations like UNESCO, which has helped increase global literacy rates from 42% in 1960 to 85% in 2016, and the World Health Organization (WHO), which has made significant strides in combating diseases such as smallpox and polio, demonstrate the UN's capacity for positive impact. However, others countered that the ongoing conflicts in Ukraine, Yemen, and Syria, which have resulted in over 100 million displaced people worldwide, have severely eroded public trust in the UN's ability to effectively address global issues and maintain peace.

West Asia crisis:
Implications and way forward

The group discussion, part of the Samwad series, focused on the ongoing conflicts in the Gulf region and the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. Participants highlighted the escalating tensions between Iran and Saudi Arabia, the humanitarian crisis in Yemen, and the ongoing Israeli-Palestinian conflict, which has resulted in over 5,000 Palestinian deaths since 2015. The discussion also touched on the implications of these conflicts on regional and global stability, including the impact on global oil prices, refugee flows, and the spread of extremist ideologies. The group emphasized the need for a multilateral approach to address the crisis, involving regional and international stakeholders, to find a peaceful resolution and promote sustainable development in the region.

Artificial Intelligence -
threat to humanity

The debate centered around the potential risks and benefits of artificial intelligence, with some participants citing warnings from prominent figures such as Stephen Hawking, Elon Musk, and Nick Bostrom about the dangers of creating a super-intelligent AI that could surpass human control. They argued that the development of AI could lead to job displacement, bias and discrimination, and existential risks if not properly managed. However, others countered that AI has the potential to augment human capabilities, solve complex problems, and improve quality of life, and that the probability of creating a superintelligent AI in the near future is low, with many experts estimating that it may take decades or even centuries to achieve such a milestone.

Climate Change and Development

The discussion centered around the relationship between climate change and development. Some argued that human activities, such as industrialization and consumption, are the primary drivers of climate change, leading to emissions of greenhouse gases. Others countered that climate change is a natural phenomenon, citing past ice ages and geological events, and that humans are simply accelerating a natural process.

Woman Reservation Bill 2023

The discussion centered around the proposed Women Reservation Bill, with some arguing that it is a necessary step to address the sufferings of women in India's patriarchal society, where they face discrimination and marginalization in areas such as education, employment, and property rights. Others countered that the bill may not benefit the intended recipients, as it may be misused by those who do not genuinely need it, and that it could lead to undue advantage and corruption. They also raised concerns that the bill could be exploited by men who claim to be women or women who are already in positions of power, and that it may create new forms of inequality and further divide society.
